Non veg diet chart for adult

Indian non vegitarian foods for weight loss for adult:

1.calories required:Calorie needs vary depending on age, sex, height and activity level. Calorie needs are often higher during the teenage years than any other time of life. During this period of rapid growth and development, boys require an average of 2,800 calories a day, while girls require an average of 2,200 calories a day.

2.Early morning

It is suggested to take lukewarm water in the early morning on an empty stomach. A cup of Tulsi tea without sugar alongside overnight soaked almond (5-7 pieces) and a walnut.

3.Breakfast 8:00 am

For breakfast, the best combination is to take a cup of low-fat milk (200 ml), 2 boiled eggs with 2 pieces of multigrain roti/bread.

4.Lunch 11:00 am

For a non-veg lunch diet, 1 and ½ cup of rice with ½ cup of Daal(lentil/mung/chana), 1 cup of mixed vegetables, 3-4 pieces of chicken meat, 1 cup of low-fat yogurt, alongside a salad.

5.Snacks 1:00pm

1 piece of any seasonal fruit, 1 slice of cheese, and a cup of green/herbal tea.

6.Tiffin 3:00 pm

For the afternoon tiffin, 2 fistfuls of beaten rice or 2 pieces of multigrain roti/bread, ½ cup of sprouted beans or low-fat yogurt, and 3-4 pieces of chicken.

7.Dinner 7:00 pm

For dinner, 1 and ½ a cup of rice or 3 pieces of roti, ½ cup of Daal(lentil/mung/chana) with 1 cup of mixed vegetables.

8. 9:00 pm

It is very beneficial to take 1 cup of low-fat milk with 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder.

If you are hungry the foods to eat are :

If you are getting hungry you can eat salad (cucumber, carrot, radish), lemon water, coconut water, green tea, black tea without sugar, turmeric tea, fresh vegetable soup, buttermilk as per your wish. But limit the lemon water, turmeric water, and green tea (not more than two cups).
